Spicy Triggerfish Salad

A zesty, tuna-salad-like mix with a kick of heat, perfect for sandwiches or over rice.

Ingredients (makes about 2 cups, serves 4–6):

  • 1 lb queen triggerfish fillets, minced
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise (or Greek yogurt for lighter option)
  • 1 tbsp sriracha or hot sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup finely diced celery
  • 2 tbsp finely diced red onion
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro or parsley
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fish: Steam or poach the triggerfish fillets for 5–7 minutes until just cooked through (internal temp 145°F). Let cool, then mince finely by hand or in a food processor.
  2. Mix ingredients: In a large bowl, combine minced fish, mayonnaise, sriracha, mustard, celery, onion, cilantro, smoked paprika, and lemon juice. Stir until evenly mixed.
  3. Season: Add salt and pepper to taste. Adjust heat with more sriracha if desired.
  4. Chill: Transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to meld flavors.
  5. Serve: Spoon over rice, stuff into a sandwich with lettuce and tomato, or serve with crackers.

Storage: Keeps in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Do not freeze (mayonnaise can separate).

Tips: For sandwiches, toast the bread to prevent sogginess. Add diced jalapeño for extra heat.

Garlic-Lime Triggerfish Dip

A creamy, garlicky spread that’s versatile for rice bowls or sandwiches, with a bright lime finish.

Ingredients (makes about 2 cups, serves 4–6):

  • 1 lb queen triggerfish fillets, minced
  • 1/3 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 2 tbsp sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tsp lime zest
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped green onion
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh dill or parsley
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fish: Steam or poach the triggerfish for 5–7 minutes until fully cooked (145°F). Cool, then mince finely.
  2. Blend base: In a bowl, mix cream cheese and sour cream until smooth. Add garlic, lime juice, and lime zest; stir well.
  3. Add fish: Fold in minced fish, green onion, dill, and smoked paprika until evenly combined.
  4. Season: Add salt and pepper to taste. Adjust with more lime juice for tanginess.
  5. Chill: Transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to set.
  6. Serve: Spread on rice with a side of veggies or use as a sandwich filling with cucumber slices.

Storage: Keeps in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Stir before serving if separation occurs.

Tips: For a smoother dip, pulse the mixture in a food processor. Serve with pita bread or crackers for variety.

Curried Triggerfish Patties

Flavorful, mildly spiced patties with a tuna-croquette vibe, perfect for rice bowls or sandwiches with a tangy sauce.

Ingredients (makes about 8 patties, serves 4–6):

  • 1 lb queen triggerfish fillets, minced
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (panko or regular)
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1/4 cup finely diced onion
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tbsp curry powder (mild or medium, adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger (or 1/2 tsp ground ginger)
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise (or Greek yogurt)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2–3 tbsp vegetable oil (for cooking)
  • Optional: 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper for heat

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fish: Steam or poach the triggerfish fillets for 5–7 minutes until just cooked through (145°F). Let cool, then mince finely by hand or pulse 3–5 times in a food processor for a tuna-like texture.
  2. Mix ingredients: In a large bowl, combine minced fish, breadcrumbs, egg, onion, cilantro, curry powder, turmeric, ginger, garlic, lime juice, mayonnaise, and cayenne (if using). Mix until it holds together when pressed. If too wet, add 1–2 tbsp more breadcrumbs; if too dry, add 1 tsp water or mayo.
  3. Form patties: Divide mixture into 8 equal portions (about 2 tbsp each). Shape into small patties, about 2 inches wide and 1/2 inch thick.
  4. Cook patties: Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook patties in batches, 3–4 minutes per side, until golden and crispy. Add more oil if needed. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Season: Sprinkle lightly with salt and pepper while warm.
  6. Chill: Let cool completely, then store in an airtight container in the fridge.
  7. Serve: Reheat gently in a skillet or microwave. Serve over rice with a drizzle of yogurt or chutney, or in a sandwich with lettuce, tomato, and a spread like mayo or mango chutney.

Storage: Keeps in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Freeze for up to 1 month (layer with parchment paper); thaw in fridge before reheating.

Tips: For sandwiches, try a brioche bun or pita for extra flavor. Pair with a side of cucumber raita for rice bowls. To keep patties firm, chill them for 15 minutes before cooking.
